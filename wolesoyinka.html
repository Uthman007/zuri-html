Prof. Wole soyinka

The Nobel laureate

Here's a time line of Professor Wole soyinka:

  • 1934 - born in Abeokuta, Nigeria
  • 1952 - 1954 - Soyinka received a primary school education in Abeokuta and then completed secondary school at Government College, Ibadan. He attended the University of Ibadan for two years before completing his undergraduate education at the University of Leeds in England in 1957.
  • 1965 - Soyinka’s reputation as the country’s leading dramatist and equally a political critic of the government was well established.
  • 1967 - he secretly and unofficially met with Ibo leaders in a vain attempt to prevent the Nigerian Civil War. When the war began Soyinka was declared a traitor and was incarcerated for the remainder of the war.
  • 1975 - he had returned to Africa and lived briefly in Accra, Ghana, where he edited a literary magazine, Transition.
  • 1986 - he was the first African writer to win a Nobel Prize in Literature
  • 2007 - he called for the cancellation of Nigerian elections due to widespread violence and fraud.
  • 1975-1999 - Soyinka was a Professor of Comparative literature at the Obafemi Awolowo University, then called the University of Ife.
  • 1999 - With civilian rule restored to Nigeria he was made professor emeritus.
  • 2008 - Soyinka was also a Distinguished Scholar in Residence at Duke University.
